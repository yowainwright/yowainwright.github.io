Builder Aspirations History

Professionaly, I build User Interfaces (UI's) and Front End Logic for web pages and applications. I like to build things a lot and learn about how things work from start to finish beyond the computer. I enjoy design, cats, cereal and do art projects when I have a little free time. I'm very into running and rock climbing which leads me to have wilderness adventures.

Building things

When I was young I would watch my dad maticulously build things with his hands. The process of turning a log of wood become a dresser or table was fasinating to me.

I am a life long creator of things. I studied painting for most of my life which led me to learning to air brush, sculpt, make furniture and design for print. It wasn't until I took a web design class in 2006-7 that I started to code. I was immediately hooked. At the time I was finishing up another degree in Design but the majority of my efforts went to building websites.

In my transition from art and design I started to build webpages for all of my design projects. I started building webpages using flash and mainly the visual portion of dreamweaver. This lead to my first job working on a PHP based site where I was mainly doing slice and dice designs and email.

My desire to learn led me to building boutique websites using dreamweaver and wordpress from small clients around Chicago. After doing this for a year, I moved to DC and was given the opportunity to design and build the front end framework for NASDAQ.com. It was a giant leap and led to me building a lot of my high traffic work; a lot of my code base is still used now.

Aspirations

My goal is to continue to learn and understand front end development, online marketing and web + application design. Eventually I would like to naturally get into consultant and product work.

I have done the majority of my professional work with .NET but for a long time I built all of my side projects using wordpress. I've had exposure to CQ5, Sitecore and a little Drupal as well. I am now getting into Ruby a bit. This site is built on Jekyll.

Within each job I've taken on I've learned that keeping everything as simple as possible is a key to success and working with a team. I like to focus on coding as modularly as possible and try to make sure work is clear and documented.

History

Born in Rapid City, South Dakota, I spent a lot of my youth in the Black Hills not imagining much beyond the small community I was in. My parents (mom) went into Diplomacy when I was 7 and my small town roots were taken international. Travelling and living abroad made me very aware of the differences between cultures and the similarities of people which I feel plays a big part in my work as a Designer and Developer.

I moved to Barbados when I was 8 and discovered table tennis. I spent to next years training to become one of the top young Bajun players along with also playing tennis competitively, body boarding and building forts.

At 13, I was living in Prague. I enjoyed the culture and the old parts of the town. We travelled a lot as a family and I got to see a lot of Central Europe.

After moving back to the United States for my Junior and Senior Year of High School, I went to college at the University of Wisconsin at Madison. While there, I spent my holidays in Albania, Bosnia and Italy with my parents work.

Learning a lot in my travels and diverse culture made me interested in communicating as an individual but also working within a team. I find a key ingredient in my work is finding where my individuality is successful and also where I can be uniform to my team.
